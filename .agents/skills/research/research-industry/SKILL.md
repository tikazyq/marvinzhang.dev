# Industry Research

Market context, trends, and competitive landscape analysis.

## When to Use

- Understanding market context for a technology or approach
- Analyzing adoption trends and industry direction
- Comparing competing tools or frameworks in the market
- Building context for trend pieces or industry analysis articles
- Standalone market evaluation (no article required)

## Research Process

### 1. Define Landscape

- **Domain**: What industry segment or technology category?
- **Timeframe**: Current snapshot, historical trend, or future projection?
- **Stakeholders**: Who are the key players, users, and decision-makers?

### 2. Data Collection

| Data Type | Sources | What to Capture |
| --------- | ------- | --------------- |
| Market data | Analyst reports, surveys, usage stats | Adoption numbers, growth rates, market share |
| Expert opinions | Conference talks, interviews, podcasts | Key predictions, consensus vs. contrarian views |
| Case studies | Company blogs, post-mortems, success stories | Real-world outcomes, scale, challenges |
| Competitive landscape | Product comparisons, feature matrices | Differentiation, positioning, trade-offs |
| Community signals | GitHub stars/activity, StackOverflow trends, Reddit | Developer sentiment, adoption velocity |

### 3. Trend Analysis

For each identified trend:
- **Evidence**: What data supports this trend?
- **Drivers**: Why is this happening now?
- **Implications**: What does this mean for practitioners?
- **Counter-signals**: What might reverse or slow this trend?
- **Timeline**: Early stage, mainstream, or mature?

### 4. Synthesis

Organize into:
- **Current state**: Where things stand today (with data)
- **Direction**: Where things are heading (with evidence)
- **Key players**: Who's driving change and how
- **Opportunities & risks**: What practitioners should watch

```markdown
|--------|------------|-----------|------------|

## Outlook
[Where this is heading, with evidence]

## Sources
[Linked, dated sources]
```

## Quality Gates

- [ ] All data points dated (no undated market claims)
- [ ] Multiple perspectives represented (not single-source)
- [ ] Primary sources preferred over secondary reporting
- [ ] Competitive analysis is balanced (not promotional)
- [ ] Counter-trends and risks acknowledged
- [ ] Practical implications for the target audience stated
